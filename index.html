Research

Working papers

PE Lobbying and Fund Performance

with Alexander Borisov and Da Li

This project studies the economic consequences of corporate political activity in private markets by examining whether General Partner lobbying affects Private Equity fund performance. It contrasts a value-enhancement hypothesis with an agency-cost hypothesis to assess which channel dominates in fund-level outcomes.

Freedom of Speech and Market-Based Corporate Governance: Evidence from Forced CEO Turnover

with Wenrui Zhang and Lu Wang

This project studies whether Anti-SLAPP laws strengthen market-based CEO discipline by improving the information environment used by boards and outside investors. Using staggered state adoption of Anti-SLAPP laws between 2007 and 2020 in a stacked difference-in-differences design, we document that forced CEO turnover becomes more sensitive to stock returns after adoption.
